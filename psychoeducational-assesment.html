Psychoeducational Assessment

What is a Psychoeducational Assessment?

A psychoeducational assessment is a comprehensive evaluation that interrogates varying domains of functioning in order to ascertain a true reflection of a student’s overall level of functioning and achievement. Areas examined typically include cognition, academic, social, and personal development:

  • Cognitive (intelligence) Assessment evaluates areas of cognition to include memory, verbal comprehension, and processing speed.
  • Academic Assessment evaluates core academic skills including reading, written expression, and mathematics.
  • Personal/Social (adaptive) Assessment evaluates areas such as communication, daily living skills, and socialization skills.

Why Consider a Psychoeducational Assessment?

These evaluations are necessary for students who are experiencing significant learning or have behavioural difficulties and require school placement, special education services, targeted learning strategies, or referral to other professional agencies. These assessments are a key diagnostic tool for identifying disorders which significantly affect a child’s typical development and/or functioning:

  • Learning Disabilities (e.g. Dyslexia)
  • Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD)
  • Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
  • Intellectual Disability

The detailed assessment is equally valuable for identifying high-achieving students and those who struggle with or have learning or behavioural disorders.

How Are Assessment Findings Utilized?

The process results in a comprehensive psycho-educational report highlighting the student’s strengths and weaknesses along with detailed recommendations. The findings from the assessment, outlined in the report, may qualify a child for various services including special accommodations and modifications that may be required in school, as well as for national and regional examinations based on the child’s identified needs. The report is accepted by the Ministry of Education, special education schools, and other agencies that provide services for children and may be a requirement for accessing these services.
